Marshall JCM2000 TSL 100 

Triple Super Lead 

100W All Valve Amplifier 

(4) EL34 Power-Amp Valves

(1) 12AX87 (EC833) Pre-Amp Valve 


Marshall 1960AV 

4x12 Speaker Cabinet 

(4) 12’ Celestion Vintage 30’s

The main focal point of my sound is my Marshall JCM2000 TSL100 All Valve Head. It features (4) EL34’s in its power-amp and (4) ECC83’s in its pre-amp putting out 100W power. The TSL is unique in that it is a JCM2000 DSL with a separate higher gain channel. It features full equalization, reverb, gain, contour, presence, and volume for each of its three channels. It also uniquely features a mid-scoop and mid-boost button for both the crunch and lead channel. It also includes a treble boost button on the clean channel. The amplifier is absolutely amazing, it  arguably has the best clean channel of any Marshall, ever… The treble boost button opens up an entire new world of brilliance, brightness, and glistening cleans I’ve yet to hear matched out of any Marshall. The crunch channel channel can easily cover a Plexi, JCM800, JCM900, and JCM2000. The “3rd Channel”, or Smaug, as I refer to it, is not really a lead channel, more of a  hard rock/heavy metal fire breathing dragon. It really pushed the envelope of Marshall sound harder than even the JVM series of amplifiers that replaced it. Marshall threw everything it had at the time into this bad mother to compete with American Hi-Gain amplifiers that were released in the early 90’s and this was probably borderline too aggressive for most Marshall Fanboys so they tamed it down in the JVM series and split the channel in two instead. This of course, is one of the defining reasons why this is my favorite amp of any Marshall. It features V.P.R. Button or Virtual Power Reduction mode feature, which simulates a reduction to only 25W of power while maintaining valve characteristics. This is paired with a Marshall 1960AV 4x12 Cabinet loaded with Celestion Vintage 30’s. I honestly don’t imagine I would ever use another speaker cabinet, except possibly an Orange cabinet someday. It’s next to impossible to beat the sound of this speaker cabinet, across any genre of music. I know for a fact, Celestion Vintage 30’s are the only speakers I would ever play through. Either recording or playing live, this is undoubtedly the sound that embodies my tonality in such a dynamic manner. The speaker can only be described as bold, loud, and articulate with strong equalization across the entire spectrum. Seriously, there is a reason why most rock musicians heavily favor this speaker, it’s just, simply put, better than anything else. This combination is, without saying, incredibly versatile and can handle essentially anything you can imagine. It is without question my favorite amplifier, and is the backbone of my entire tonality.  

Marshall Valvestate AVT50

50W Hybrid Tube Amplifier Combo

12” Custom Celestion Speaker

(1) 12AX87 (EC833) Pre-Amp Valve 

20W Class A/B Solid State Power-Amp

I occasionally make use of a Marshall AVT50, in some circumstances. It is a hybrid value amplifier and offers unique sound very different from either a solid state “MG” series or all valve “premium” Marshall amplifiers. The AVT50 features a single 12AX7(ECC83) Valve in the pre-amp and a 50W class D solid state power-amp. It features 2 foot switchable channels with separate equalization and gain, spring reverb, auxiliary input, headphone output, emulated line out, fully buffered effects-loop, speaker output, and a single 12” built-in custom version of a Celestion Vintage 30 speaker. In my experience, the AVT series of amplifiers are extremely underrated, and undeniably produced a tone years ahead of its time. The amplifiers produce some the the most bass heavy, saturated, and overdriven sounds I’ve ever heard come out of any Marshall. I have the TSL 100 for side-by-side direct comparison, and the AVT50 sounds pretty damn good folks. It completely blows away any similar Marshall MG series amplifier. I’m not understating that either, the Marshall AVT series is another model I’m lucky to own as it is produced anymore. It may be viewed as slightly too aggressive sounding for individuals looking for a Plexi model tone, which is why it has negative reviews and opinions. However for those of us who don’t even consider a Plexi models overdrive to considered “distortion”. 

Orange Terror Stamp

20W Hybrid Tube Amplifier 

(1) 12AX87 (EC833) Pre-Amp Valve20W 20W Class A/B Solid State Power-Amp

I also use Orange Amplifiers for a more modkkern range of sound. Specifically the Orange Terror Stamp and Orange Crush20. The Terror Stamp is a 20W Hybrid Valve amplifier featuring a single 12AX7(ECC83) Valve in the pre-amp and a 20W Class A/B amplifier in the power-amp section. It has a fully-buffered effects-loop, a cab sim output, and two foot-switchable volume level settings. The tone generated from this tiny pedal amplifier is now, without question, my favorite sound and the reason I’ve been moving towards Orange from Marshall lately. Words I would have never thought I’d ever use 20+ years ago, however I can’t find any excuse to not be impressed. This cheap Orange Terror , in addition to the JOYO BanTamP ZoMBe II, are now my main recording amplifiers. That’s correct, much like the latest “cheap” guitars I’ve purchased, these “cheap” valve hybrid amps are simply something you didn’t have 20+ years ago. Orange has had resurgence because they listen to customers changing tone needs, and give them many different options to choose from. Orange has always had an iconic sound, however scaling it down to the size of a pedal, and still blowing it out of the water is seriously impressive. This “pedal” single-handedly made me an Orange Fanboy now, and my next amp will probably be the all valve dual terror to be honest. 

Orange Crush 20

20W Analog Solid State 

Combo Amplifier 

4-Stage Gain Pre-Amp

(1) 8 “ Voice of the World Speaker

 In addition I use an Orange Crush20 all analog solid-state amplifier. This inexpensive combo amplifier has unbelievable sound, and features a 4-stage gain pre-amp that produces all sorts of flavors of famous Orange sound. It features 2 foot switchable channels, cab sim output, 3-Band EQ, separate volume settings, and a custom 8” Orange “Voice of the World” speaker. Neither of these amps will satisfy the taste of “tube snobs”, but that doesn’t mean anything. This thing sounds great good, almost too good compared to anything in its class right now, including Marshall’s MG series. This small solid state amp manages to pull off some amazing tones, and is now my primary practice amp amp. Frankly, I don’t see how/why anyone would/could need anything more for practicing at home, alone. I absolutely love the simplicity of Oranges philosophy on the clean channel, only a volume knob. The sound is perfectly balanced, and even begins to simulate some interesting valve characteristics at high volume (Clipping and Compression). The 4-Stage pre-amp on the dirty channel gives you 4 really useful amounts of gain, from blues, to classic rock, to hard rock, all the way up Slipknot. That my kind of amp if there ever was one, that’s for damn sure. I mentioned Slipknot because, Jim Root, has long been a vocal supporter and ambassador for orange amps. JOYO BantamP XL 

ZoMBe II

20W Hybrid Valve Amplifier 

(1) 12AX87 (EC833) Pre-Amp Valve

20W Class D Solid State Power-Amp

Recently I have introduced the JOYO Zombie-II (Dual Rectifier) BanTamp XL Series to my setup. This is a 20W hybrid valve amplifier that features a single 12AX7(ECC83) Valve in the pre-amp and a 20W Class D Solid-State power-amp. It features two foot switchable channels, cab sim output, fully-buffered effects-loop, and built in Bluetooth. Designed to mimic the sound of a Mess Boogie Dual Rectifier, it has massive amounts of gain at its disposal. Although it obviously does not sound identical to a Mesa Boogie, it features a raw metal sound that is unique to itself. The sound can best be described as a combination of Orange,  Mesa Boogie, and German amps, yet with higher gain, seriously. This thing will Chugg Hard by 4-5 on the gain, which is seriously impressive, if you like that stuff, I do. With some proper pedals, noise gate, compression, overdrive, etc…to /further sculpt the sound it becomes absolute beastly. Simply put, watch the F*** out for this little guy to be shredding harder than your $10,000 custom amp. Try not to be too butthurt about it, it’s ok, I was at first too. I was looking to incorporate a uniquely distinct approach and alternate to Mesa Boogie, and I’m happy I did. I use this in combination with my Orange Terror Stamp, using an AB/Y box , with some compression, noise gate, and an Ibanez tube screamer in front, to create a signature sound that is truly sinister.  I’m not talking about some cheesy “scary movie” you take your girlfriend too, I’m talking about Saron himself putting on a concert with his band of 9 Ring Wraiths backing him up, for all the Orks at the base of Mt. Doom in Mordor kind of Darkness over here folks. If I had to describe the someone what kind of sound I want to achieve, that would be it in a nutshell. Call it “1 of 9” if you want, get on my level. 

Crate GFX-65T

65W Solid State Combo

Crate Designed 12” Speaker

Crate GX900H

90W Solid State Amplifier

3 Channel

5th Gen Crate Flex-wave Pre-Amp 

Crate GX414A Speaker Cabinet

[4] Crate Designed 12” Speakers

Max RMS 280W

In 1990, Crate patented a solid state technology that simulates the sound of a hard-driven tube circuit. They called it FlexWave, and these amps feature FlexWave Evolution Five Preamp, the 5th generation of its technological advances. And the GFX line of FlexWave amps couple that same tone with 10 built in digital effects which include Flange, Chorus, Delay, Reverb, and Chorus + Reverb, etc. The GX900H is a 90W solid state amplifier head, and the GFX-65T is a 65W Combo. Both amps feature three foot switchable channels with full equalization for clean and dirty channels, a fully buffered effects loop, and separate volume, shape, and gain settings for every channel. These amps where my “stepping stones” and are fantastic amplifiers that I still play to this day. The clean channel on these is absolutely fantastic, shimmering with pure clarity. The overdrive channels are intense, with lots of saturation, girth, brightness, tightness, and low end punch. They both remind me of solid state Mesa Boogie Triple Rectifiers in every sense imaginable. The 4x12 cabinet is also excellent, with a metal grill that looks absolutely amazing, mean, and intimidating. That custom Crate 12” speakers sound very similar to Celestion Vintage 30’s with stronger presence, tightness, and lower bottom end. In practice today I use this 4x12 Cab with my JOYO BanTamP XL ZoMBe II and it’s really a perfect match. If you think that a 20W hybrid amp through a 4x12 isn’t loud enough to cut through the mix, you are wrong. I can barley move the volume on the amp past 1-2 without it being too loud for the house. When it’s cranked, it absolutely drowns out the band, let alone cut through the mix. Being a multi-instrumentalist and being a drummer first and foremost, I play the drums HARD, LOUD, and PASSIONATELY and if it can cut through me on the kicks, it will cut through anyone. Trust me.
